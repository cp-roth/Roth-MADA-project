1.6 Presentation

How well are results presented? Are tables and figures easy to read and understand? Are the main figures/tables publication level quality?

The results were presented very well. The only issue I had with any of the tables or figures was the length of table 1.

A couple of issues to look at:

In the word document, table 3 seems to be off the page on the left. Same thing for Table 5.

The figure captions need to be included in the figure captions. For example, for figure 3 you have “Scatterplot of birth weight by length” in the code after “fig-cap:”, then you add a completely different figure caption: “Figure 3 visualizes the relationship between infant weight and length. Unsurprisingly, birth weight and length are positively correlated.” Combine these. Also, it is traditional to have the figure description below the figure. Just a suggestion, but it would make the paper come together a bit better.

I would also take some of the page breaks out. They are a bit distracting. For example, the huge gap between the abstract and the introduction leaves quite a bit of scrolling down. Smoother pagination makes a paper flow easier.

Again, all of this is just a suggestion. The paper looks pretty good as it is.
